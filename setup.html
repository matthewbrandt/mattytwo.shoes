Setup

Here is a list of most of the hardware and software that I use as part of my work, programming and content creation workflow!

home

Computer & Accessories

2023 Apple Mac Mini M2 Pro 12 Core, 32 GB, 500GB SSD

The Mac Mini was a logical upgrade from my 2019 MacBook Pro. Streaming, recording and editing video is no problem on this beast!

Samsung SyncMaster C43J890DKU 43" Ultrawide

I use a curved ultrawide as my main monitor as it allows me to view lots of things without a break in immersion.

HP Compaq LA2306x LED Backlit LCD-Monitor

An old relic from my parents, it's a useful third monitor that I have mounted vertically.

AOC U34G3XM 34" Ultrawide

It's quite unusual to have such a wide monitor in vertical orientation, but it's very useful for me to have an overview on various communication tools (i.e. Slack, Discord, Telegram etc.).

Godox GM55 Camera monitor

Connected to my main camera, so that I can see what my camera sees (since the flip screen is to tiny). Useful for knowing if I am in focus, if brightness is peaking anywhere, etc.

Apple iPad 9th Gen

As an additional screen for work and for drawing on my stream I have this standard iPad. It's especially useful for having Discord running during the day when hanging out in co-working channels as the call doesn't occupy any of my audio/video devices on my Mac Mini!

Input Devices

TIE Condensor USB Microphone

One of the most affordable USB microphones on the market. When starting out, there isn't any need to go all-out on gear! Given my current setup, I'll be looking to change to a shotgun microphone sometime soon.

Logitech MX Keys for Mac

Logitech makes some of the most affordable gear. I've had this keyboard for many years and the weight makes it nice to type on. The wireless charge has started to dwindle to a couple days but it's USB-C so can be charged easily.

Logitech MX Master 3

This mouse is wildly popular for a reason - it's just so good! I am now on my third or fourth Logitech MX mouse and I would probably only consider the vertical one at this point if I was to change.

Canon R6 (mirrorless)

I've had Canon cameras for a very long time and this is my third camera body in around 12 years, but also my first mirrorless. I power the camera with a "dummy" DC battery plugged into the wall so that it doesn't turn off and have it plugged into a camera monitor.

Canon EF 40mm f/2.8 STM

While I own quite a few lenses, I mainly use this "pancake" lens for at the desk (together with an RF adapter). The field of view is relatively narrow, which allows me to easily place things out of view (like the microphone).

Logitech Brio 4k

As a backup camera (currently in the desk overview position) I use my old trusty webcam. It has decent exposure and lighting but the autofocus can be quite troublesome. I do apply some filters to make it look a little better and that's more than sufficient for its purpose!

Elgato Stream Deck (MK1)

Enough has been written about these amazing macropads that I don't need to say much! For automating switching scenes while live, triggering sounds or changing lights and even when editing in Davinci Resolve, this handy little LED-keypad is really neat.

Desk & Chair

Yaasa Desk Pro 2

Courtesy of Yaasa, I received a Desk Pro 2 to build live on stream! I've had the desk for several months now and it's the perfect desk for me. As a sit-stand desk it moves smoothly and quickly and when extended it's still stable enough to not shake my entire setup!

Lighting

Philips Hue Bulbs

In order to light up my background, I use a pair of Hue E27 bulbs (that's right, it's not a greenscreen). It's surprising how much power they can deliver. If you have seen my setup photos, you know the lights are attached to a desk frame and are pointed straight at the wall behind me.

Elgato Key Light Air

These lights (2x) are for lighting up me in front of the camera. They can be controlled wirelessly which makes setting them up and changing the settings really easy. Obviously they pair really well with the Elgato Stream Deck but you can also use their free mobile app to adjust them too.

Eurolite PAR 38

As mentioned, the Philips Hue bulbs are mounted in these metal "stage" lights, which sit directly above my head, pointing behind me. The best part is, I am able to use filters in front of the lights to cast different shapes, if I wanted to! They're pretty unique, so I provided a link below.
